Degree Levels Available: Associate, bachelors, masters, Ph.D. Popular Specializations: Construction engineering and management, transportation engineering, geotechnical engineering, structural engineering Common Career Paths: Civil engineer, transportation engineer, geotechnical engineer Major Overview: Civil engineers plan, design, develop and execute building and infrastructure projects. Civil engineering majors learn how to solve complex engineering problems and apply solutions in geotechnical, structural, transportation and civil engineering contexts. Civil engineers usually require a bachelors degree and earn a median income of $89,940 annually.
